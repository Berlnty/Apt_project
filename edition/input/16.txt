keyboards, vocals</td></tr></table><p><b>Plants and Animals</b> are a Canadian <a href="/wiki/Indie-rock" class="mw-redirect" title="Indie-rock">indie-rock</a> band from <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> (featuring two members originally from <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Regional_Municipality" class="mw-redirect" title="Halifax Regional Municipality">Halifax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a>) which comprises guitarist-vocalists Warren Spicer and Nic Basque and drummer-vocalist Matthew Woody Woodley.<sup id="cite_ref-exclaimmag_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-exclaimmag-1">[1]</a></sup> The trio began playing together as kids and emerged on the international scene in 2008. They are signed to <a href="/wiki/Secret_City_Records" title="Secret City Records">Secret City Records</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p><p>Their first full-length album, <i>Parc Avenue</i>, was shortlisted for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Polaris_Music_Prize" title="2008 Polaris Music Prize">2008 Polaris Music Prize</a> and was nominated for a <a href="/wiki/Juno_Awards_of_2009" title="Juno Awards of 2009">2009 Juno Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Juno_Award_for_Alternative_Album_of_the_Year" title="Juno Award for Alternative Album of the Year">Alternative Album of the Year</a>; the band were also nominated for <a href="/wiki/Juno_Award_for_New_Group_of_the_Year" class="mw-redirect" title="Juno Award for New Group of the Year">New Group of the Year</a>.</p><p>In February 2016, the band announced the upcoming release of their fourth full-length album <a href="/wiki/Waltzed_in_from_the_Rumbling" title="Waltzed in from the Rumbling">Waltzed in from the Rumbling</a> that will come out April 29, 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-waltzedannounce_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-waltzedannounce-3">[3]</a></sup></p><p></p><div id="toc" class="toc"><div class="toctitle" lang="en" dir="ltr"><h2>Contents</h2></div><ul><li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1"><a links</span></a></li></ul></div><p></p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Plants_and_Animals&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p>Plants and Animals' self-titled EP was released in 2003 via Ships at Night Records.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><p>In the fall of 2007, Plants and Animals released the four-song <i>with/avec</i> EP</p><p>Their full-length debut <i>Parc Avenue</i> was released on February 26, 2008 in Canada and on March 25, 2008 in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-parclife_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-parclife-5">[5]</a></sup> The album was recorded entirely on analogue tape and features string parts by <a href="/wiki/Sarah_Neufeld" title="Sarah Neufeld">Sarah Neufeld</a> of <a href="/wiki/Arcade_Fire" title="Arcade Fire">Arcade Fire</a></p><p>The band released their second LP, titled <i>La La Land</i>, on April 20, 2010. The album was recorded in both The Treatment Room in the band's hometown of Montreal and La Frette Studios outside of Paris, France.</p><p>Their third album, <i>The End of That</i>, was released on February 28, 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-endofthat_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-endofthat-6">[6]</a></sup> The trio spent nearly a year preparing a catalogue of songs before entering the studio.<sup id="cite_ref-ifplantsandanimalswere_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ifplantsandanimalswere-7">[7]</a></sup></p><h2><span class="mw-headline"
