13 Humans Now in Orbit</h1>					<p class="headline_meta">by <span class="author vcard fn">Nancy Atkinson</span> on <abbr class="published" title="2009-03-26">March 26, 2009</abbr></p>				</div>				<div class="format_text entry-content"><p><div id="attachment_27972" class="wp-caption aligncenter" style="width: 351px">	<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/27924/soyuz-rockets-to-space-13-humans-now-in-orbit/soyuz-launch-bill-ingalls6/" rel="attachment wp-att-27972"><img src="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720im_/http://www.universetoday.com/wp-content/uploads/2009/03/soyuz-launch-bill-ingalls6-351x580.jpg" alt="Soyuz launch.  Credit:  Bill Ingalls/NASA" title="Soyuz launch.  Credit:  Bill Ingalls/NASA" width="351" height="580" class="size-medium wp-image-27972"/></a>	<p class="wp-caption-text">Soyuz launch.  Credit:  Bill Ingalls/NASA</p></div><br/>It&#8217;s a busy <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/14700/how-long-is-a-day-on-earth/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">day</a> in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/25406/space/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">space</a>, with 13 humans now in <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/34665/orbit/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">orbit</a>.  A Soyuz rocket blasted off amid clouds and drizzle at the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/36948/baikonur-cosmodrome/">Baikonur Cosmodrome</a> today (Thursday) sending  Russian <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/36845/cosmonaut/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">cosmonaut</a> Gennady Padalka , American <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/49018/astronauts/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">astronaut</a> Michael Barratt,and American <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/32729/spaceflight/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">spaceflight</a> participant Charles Simonyi on their way to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/52067/international-space-station/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">International Space Station</a>. With lift-off at 11:49 a.m. GMT, Simonyi made history by becoming the first private explorer to make a second trip to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/52067/international-space-station/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">ISS</a>.  He previously flew to there in April 2007.  With the shuttle <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/53090/discovery-space-shuttle/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">Discovery</a> crew of seven just leaving the station on Wednesday, the current <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/26943/iss-crew-may-need-to-evacuate-possible-debris-hit/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">ISS crew</a> of three on board the station, and the Soyuz three, that totals equals the current record of 13 humans in space at one time*.</p><p>And things will just get busier:  this new ISS crew will grow to six in May.<br/><span id="more-27924"></span><br/>The Soyuz is scheduled to dock with the station at 01:14 p.m. GMT  aturday, March 28. Padalka will serve as commander of Expeditions 19 and 20 aboard the station. Barratt will serve as a flight engineer for those two missions. Padalka and Barratt&#8217;s other crewmate is Koichi Wakata of the Japan <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/38075/aerospace/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">Aerospace</a> Exploration Agency. He arrived to the station March 17 on space shuttle Discovery. </p><p>Simonyi, flying to the station under a commercial agreement with the Russian Federal Space Agency, will spend 10 days aboard the ISS, and return to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/14367/earth/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">Earth</a> April 7 with Expedition 18 Commander Michael Fincke and Flight Engineer Yury Lonchakov, who have been on the station since October 2008. </p><p>The Expedition 19 crew will continue science investigations and prepare for the arrival of the rest of the station&#8217;s first six-person contingent. Roman Romanenko of the Russian Federal Space Agency, Frank De Winne of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/78043/european-space-agency/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">European Space Agency</a> and Canadian Space Agency astronaut Bob Thirsk will launch from <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/36948/baikonur-cosmodrome/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">Baikonur</a> on May 27, arriving at the station on May 29. After all the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/49018/astronauts/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">astronauts</a> are aboard, Expedition 20 will begin, ushering in an era of six-person station crews. This mission also will be the first time the crew members represent all five International Space Station partners. </p><p>*A quick search of the times there were 13 people in space previously yielded it occurred in 1995 when STS-67  was in orbit, along with the crew of three on the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20110101151720/http://www.universetoday.com/57063/mir-space-station/" class="alinks_links" title="" rel="external">Mir space station</a> and a Soyuz in flight, and also in 1997 when STS-82 was doing a Hubble repair mission, and the Mir crew and a Soyuz crew were also in space.  I&#8217;ll update that if I find out more, and if anyone else has more information, post a comment.
