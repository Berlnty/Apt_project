In total, the local students earned an aggregate of more than $1.9 million in college scholarships from different schools.</p><div class="trb_gptAd trb_ar_main_ad" data-role="adloader adloader_ad delayload delayload_done" data-adloader-size="300x250" data-state="adloader_unloaded"  data-withinviewport-options="bottomOffset=100&topOffset=1000000"  data-adloader-position="articleCube" data-adloader-adtype="articlebottomcube" data-adloader-label ></div       ><p>Chris Sykora, an art teacher at Deerfield, is the co-director of the exhibition, which he said is a nonprofit corporation. He said the annual event showcases student art from across the state and gives seniors the opportunity to share their portfolios with colleges with the hope of earning scholarships.</p><p>Colleges and universities make offers based on the student portfolios, Sykora said. They are really starting to understand our program and its growing.</p><div class="trb_ar_cont" data-ar-cont="Article continues below"><svg class="trb_ar_cont_svg"    ><use xlink:href="#trb_thin_arrow_down"  /></svg></div>            <aside class="trb_ar_sponsoredmod" data-v-ymid="ym_1082618974440896766" data-adloader-networktype="yieldmo" data-role="delayload delayload_item" data-screen-size='mobile' data-withinviewport-options="bottomOffset=100&topOffset=1000000" data-load-method="trb.vendor.yieldmo.init" data-load-type="method" >            </aside>  <aside class="trb_ar_sponsoredmod" data-v-ntidd="1069679" data-adloader-networktype="nativo" data-role="delayload" data-withinviewport-options="bottomOffset=100" data-load-method="trb.vendor.nativo.init" data-load-type="method"          ></aside><p>A year ago, Sykora said $19 million worth of scholarships were awarded and this year the number climbed to more than $43 million. Many of the students get multiple offers before selecting one. In 2017, he said approximately 250 students took part and this year it was over 380.</p><p>Rodriguez, a painter who said starts by taking a picture of an urban scene before adding layers of paint, said he received scholarship offers from the Art Academy of Cincinnati, the Milwaukee Institute of Art and Design and the New Hampshire Institute of Art.</p><p>Its really, really crazy, Rodriguez said. I never thought my painting would lead to this kind of opportunity. Im going to have to take a road trip and see which one I like best. This just feels great.</p><p>Highland Park art teacher Blake Novotny said the exhibition not only gives students opportunities to continue their art and education, it also offers them a large show in which to display their work.</p><div class="trb_ar_cont" data-ar-cont="Article continues below"><svg class="trb_ar_cont_svg"    ><use xlink:href="#trb_thin_arrow_down"  /></svg></div>                    <aside class="trb_ar_sponsoredmod" data-v-ymid="ym_1606461062581172542" data-role="delayload" data-screen-size="mobile" data-withinviewport-options="bottomOffset=100&topOffset=1000000" data-load-method="trb.vendor.yieldmo.init" data-load-type="method"></aside><p>It gives us a nice forum and a good place for the kids to share their work, Novotny said. It also gives (younger students) a chance to get scholarships for summer programs.</p><p>While students like Rodriquez take advantage of the scholarships, others like Deerfield senior Ryan Wilde make an entirely different college choice.</p><p>Wilde said he plans to attend Drexel University in Philadelphia to major in art. Despite the art school offers, he likes the idea of combining other elements of a multifaceted university with one that has a very good graphic design program.</p><p>Along with the scholarships, Sykora said Wilde was awarded the best in show prize in the graphic arts category. The eight categories featured were drawing, painting, mixed media, design, photography, pottery, sculpture and time arts.</p><p>The show was a great way to display my art, Wilde said. It was a chance to be in a large professional (style) show.</p><p>Not all the awards and scholarships went to seniors. Deerfield sophomore Sophie Centazzo won the vision award for photography. She said she also got scholarship offers for summer study. She will be using one in July at the Savannah College of Art and Design in Georgia.</p><div class="trb_ar_cont" data-ar-cont="Article continues below"><svg class="trb_ar_cont_svg"    ><use xlink:href="#trb_thin_arrow_down"  /></svg></div><aside class="trb_em trb_embed" data-content-id="96271366" data-content-size="large" data-content-type="image" data-content-slug="ct-1522102599-o6xv0l235j-snap-image" data-content-subtype="photo"        data-role="sc_item imgsize_ratiosizecontainer lightbox_container "        data-state=""         data-embed-id="96271366">      <span class="trb_em_m_cl" data-role="lightbox_close"><svg class="trb_em_m_cl_svg"    ><use xlink:href="#trb_thin_close"  /></svg></span>    <div class="trb_em_m"  ><figure data-role=" delayload  delayload_done imgsize_item" class="trb_em_ic_figure">      <img itemprop="image" data-baseurl="http://www.trbimg.com/img-5ab9714b/turbine/ct-1522102599-o6xv0l235j-snap-image" alt="Sophie Centazzo" class="trb_em_ic_img" title="Sophie Centazzo" data-c-nd="1081x608" /></figure>  <span class="trb_em_m_op" data-role="lightbox_open"><svg class="trb_em_m_op_svg"    ><use xlink:href="#trb_arrow_expand"  /></svg></span><div class="trb_em_r" data-role="lightbox_metadata">    <span class="trb_em_r_t"  data-ri-t="Sophie Centazzo"></span><div class="trb_em_r_cr">Sophie Centazzo</div><div class="trb_em_r_ca">      Deerfield High School sophomore Sophie Centazzo stands in front of her award winning artwork at the Illinois High School Art Exhibition in Chicago.</div><div class="trb_em_r_cc">Deerfield High School sophomore Sophie Centazzo stands in front of her award winning artwork at the Illinois High School Art Exhibition in Chicago.
