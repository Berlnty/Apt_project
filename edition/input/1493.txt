	<div id="cn-centre-col-gap"></div>	<div id="cn-centre-col"><div id="cn-centre-col-inner"><!--2011.07.04.02-->				<h1 id="cn-cont">Raising elder abuse awareness through intergenerational art</h1><div class="floatLeft padRight10"><img alt="Kit Julian, left, shows a work-in-progress, while her ?Little Sister? Lori sorts through ribbon" src="/web/20130122162638im_/http://www.hrsdc.gc.ca/eng/success_stories/seniors/40/00.jpg"/></div><p>The Lighthouse Festival Theatre is a professional theatre company in <strong>Port Dover</strong>,<strong> Ontario</strong>. It hosts an all-Canadian playbill, community fundraisers, concerts, high school classes, youth programs, meetings and other special events. In addition to these events, Operation Peacock: a Celebration of Elder Vibrancy was also featured at the Lighthouse Festival Theatre. The theatre received funding for Operation Peacock through the Government of Canada&#8217;s New Horizons for Seniors Program.</p><p>The main goals of the project were to build relationships between generations, provide young people with mentors and educate the public about elder abuse and the isolation facing many seniors.</p><p>The Lighthouse Festival Theatre worked with Big Brothers Big Sisters of Grand Erie, pairing senior artists with younger artists, called &#8220;Little Brothers&#8221; and &#8220;Little Sisters,&#8221; to create original works of art. The art was then sold at a silent auction at the theatre, which raised $2,000 to support the Seniors Safety Line. The Seniors Safety Line is a toll-free, confidential resource for seniors in Ontario suffering from abuse, including financial, physical, sexual or mental abuse and neglect.</p><p>Helen Wagenaar, Administrative Director at the Lighthouse Festival Theatre, said that the theatre has a strong tie to seniors. &#8220;Lighthouse is dedicated to improving the quality of life for seniors in our community, especially when it comes to raising awareness of elder abuse,&#8221; said Helen.</p><p>Kit Julian is a former Executive Director of the Alzheimer Society of Haldimand Norfolk, an advocate for not-for-profit agencies and the artistic community and project coordinator for Operation Peacock. She said that &#8220;creating an interaction between young people and older adults, whether vulnerable or not, is a joyful, uplifting, and edifying message for the future.&#8221;</p><p>Both Helen and Kit thought the project was a tremendous success. &#8220;This project heightened awareness of the seniors&#8217; hotline. Most people we encountered did not know the hotline existed,&#8221; said Kit. &#8220;Younger people who had never interacted with seniors gained new friends and will continue to see older adults in a very different light. We could write a novel about the jokes, laughs, coincidences, kindnesses, optimism and success of this relatively small project,&#8221; she added.</p><p>&#8220;This project helped seniors connect with younger people and find common ground through art,&#8221; said Helen. &#8220;It also helped raise important issues, including the prevention of elder abuse, reducing isolation and recognizing vibrant seniors for their skills and ability to mentor younger people.&#8221;</p><p>In order to share and build upon the project&#8217;s success, the creative process was filmed, compiled and a series of video clips have been posted on YouTube to be used as a template for other communities interested in undertaking a similar project. To watch Operation Peacock, click <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130122162638/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iAAn0shmL_8">here</a>. The videos are available in English only.)</p><p>The Seniors Safety Line can be reached at 1-866-299-1011 and is operated in association with the Assaulted Women&#8217;s Help Line.</p><p>The <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20130122162638/http://www.hrsdc.gc.ca/eng/community_partnerships/seniors/index.shtml">New Horizons for Seniors Program</a> is a federal grants and contributions program that supports projects led or inspired by seniors who want to make a difference in the lives of others and in their communities.
