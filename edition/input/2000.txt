Now, researchers have found evidence that suggests the ancestors of Aboriginal Australians landed in the northern part of Australia at least 65,000 years ago.</p><p class="story-body-text story-content" data-para-count="308" data-total-count="563">The finding, which was published Wednesday in the journal <a href="https://www.nature.com/articles/doi:10.1038/nature22968">Nature</a>, pushes back the timing of when people first came to the continent by about 5,000 to 18,000 years.
