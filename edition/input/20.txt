origin of the word "travel" is most likely lost to history. The term "travel" may originate from the <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> word <i>travail</i>, which means 'work'.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> According to the Merriam Webster dictionary, the first known use of the word <i>travel</i> was in the 14th century. It also states that the word comes from Middle English <i>travailen</i>, <i>travelen</i> (which means to torment, labor, strive, journey) and earlier from Old French <i>travailler</i> (which means to work strenuously, toil). In English we still occasionally use the words "travail", which means struggle. According to Simon Winchester in his book <i>The Best Travelers' Tales (2004)</i>, the words "travel" and "travail" both share an even more ancient root: a Roman instrument of torture called the <i>tripalium</i> (in Latin it means "three stakes", as in to impale). This link may reflect the extreme difficulty of travel in ancient times. Today, travel may or may not be much easier depending upon the destination you choose (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Mt._Everest" class="mw-redirect" title="Mt. Everest">Mt. Everest</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_rainforest" title="Amazon rainforest">Amazon rainforest</a>), how you plan to get there (<a href="/wiki/Bus" title="Bus">tour bus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cruise_ship" title="Cruise ship">cruise ship</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Bullock_cart" title="Bullock cart">oxcart</a>), and whether you decide to "rough it" (see <a href="/wiki/Extreme_tourism" title="Extreme tourism">extreme tourism</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adventure_travel" title="Adventure travel">adventure travel</a>). "There's a big difference between simply being a tourist and being a true world traveler", notes travel writer Michael Kasum. This is, however, a contested distinction as academic work on the cultures and sociology of travel has noted.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p><h2><span href="/wiki/Interpersonal_relationship" title="Interpersonal relationship">interpersonal relationships</a>.</li></ul><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_travel">History of travel</span></h2><p>Travel dates back to <a href="/wiki/Classical_antiquity" title="Classical antiquity">antiquity</a> where wealthy Greeks and Romans would travel for leisure to their summer homes and villas in cities such as <a href="/wiki/Pompeii" title="Pompeii">Pompeii</a> and <a href="/wiki/Baiae" title="Baiae">Baiae</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-matador_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-matador-7">[7]</a></sup> While early travel tended to be slower, more dangerous, and more dominated by trade and migration, cultural and technological advances over many years have tended to mean that travel has become easier and more accessible.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Mankind has come a long way in transportation since <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> sailed to the new world from Spain in 1492, an expedition which took over 10 weeks to arrive at the final destination; to the 21st century where <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a> allow travel from Spain to the United States overnight.</p><p>Travel in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> offered hardships and challenges, however, it was important to the economy and to society. The <a href="/wiki/Wholesaling" title="Wholesaling">wholesale sector</a> depended (for example) on <a href="/wiki/Merchants" class="mw-redirect" title="Merchants">merchants</a> dealing with/through <a href="/wiki/Caravan_(travellers)" title="Caravan (travellers)">caravans</a> or sea-voyagers, end-user <a href="/wiki/Retailing" class="mw-redirect" title="Retailing">retailing</a> often demanded the services of many itinerant <a href="/wiki/Peddler" title="Peddler">peddlers</a> wandering from village to hamlet, <a href="/wiki/Gyrovague" title="Gyrovague">gyrovagues</a> (Wandering Monks) and wandering <a href="/wiki/Friar" title="Friar">friars</a> brought <a href="/wiki/Theology" title="Theology">theology</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pastoral_care" title="Pastoral care">pastoral support</a> to neglected areas, <a href="/wiki/Travelling_minstrel" class="mw-redirect" title="Travelling minstrel">travelling minstrels</a> practiced the never-ending tour, and armies ranged far and wide in various crusades and in sundry other wars.<sup id="cite_ref-matador_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-matador-7">[7]</a></sup> Pilgrimages were common in both the European and Islamic world and involved streams of travellers both locally (<a href="/wiki/Canterbury_Tales" class="mw-redirect" title="Canterbury Tales">Canterbury Tales</a>-style) and internationally.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></p><p>In the late 16th century it became fashionable for young European <a href="/wiki/Aristocrat" class="mw-redirect" title="Aristocrat">aristocrats</a> and wealthy upper class men to travel to significant European cities as part of their education in the arts and literature. This was known as the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Tour" title="Grand Tour">Grand Tour</a>, it included cities such as London, Paris, Venice, Florence and Rome. However, The <a href="/wiki/French_revolution" class="mw-redirect" title="French revolution">French revolution</a> brought with it the end of the Grand Tour.<sup id="cite_ref-matador_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-matador-7">[7]</a></sup></p><p>Travel by water often provided more comfort and speed than land-travel, at least until the advent of a network of <a href="/wiki/Railway" class="mw-redirect" title="Railway">railways</a> in the 19th century. Travel for the purpose of tourism is reported to have started around this time when people began to travel for fun as travel was no longer a hard and challenging task. This was capitalised on by people like <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Cook" title="Thomas Cook">Thomas Cook</a> selling tourism packages where trains and hotels were booked together.<sup id="cite_ref-DW_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DW-10">[10]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Airship" title="Airship">Airships</a> and <a href="/wiki/Airplane" title="Airplane">airplanes</a> took over much of the role of long-distance surface travel in the 20th century, notably after the second World War where there was a surplus of both aircraft and pilots.<sup id="cite_ref-matador_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-matador-7">[7]</a></sup></p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geographic_types">Geographic types</span></h2><p>Travel may be local, regional, national (domestic) or <a href="/wiki/International" title="International">international</a>. In some countries, non-local internal travel may require an <a href="/wiki/Internal_passport" title="Internal passport">internal passport</a>, while international travel typically requires a <a href="/wiki/Passport" title="Passport">passport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Visa_(document)" class="mw-redirect" title="Visa (document)">visa</a>. A trip may also be part of a round-trip, which is a particular type of travel whereby a person moves from one location to another and returns.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a importance of taking precautions to ensure travel <a href="/wiki/Safety" title="Safety">safety</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-travel.state.gov_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel.state.gov-12">[12]</a></sup> When traveling abroad, the odds favor a safe and incident-free trip, however, travelers can be subject to difficulties, crime and violence.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Some safety considerations include being aware of one's surroundings,<sup id="cite_ref-travel.state.gov_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel.state.gov-12">[12]</a></sup> avoiding being the target of a crime,<sup id="cite_ref-travel.state.gov_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel.state.gov-12">[12]</a></sup> leaving copies of one's <a href="/wiki/Passport" title="Passport">passport</a> and <a href="/wiki/Travel_itinerary" title="Travel itinerary">itinerary</a> information with trusted people,<sup id="cite_ref-travel.state.gov_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel.state.gov-12">[12]</a></sup> obtaining <a href="/wiki/Health_insurance" title="Health insurance">medical insurance</a> valid in the country being visited<sup id="cite_ref-travel.state.gov_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel.state.gov-12">[12]</a></sup> and registering with one's national <a href="/wiki/Diplomatic_mission" title="Diplomatic mission">embassy</a> when arriving in a foreign country.<sup id="cite_ref-travel.state.gov_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel.state.gov-12">[12]</a></sup> Many countries do not recognize drivers' licenses from other countries; however most countries accept <a href="/wiki/International_driving_permit" class="mw-redirect" title="International driving permit">international driving permits</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-travel.state.gov.permits_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel.state.gov.permits-14">[14]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vehicle_insurance" title="Vehicle insurance">Automobile insurance</a> policies issued in one's own country are often invalid in foreign countries, and it is often a requirement to obtain temporary auto insurance valid in the country being visited.<sup id="cite_ref-travel.state.gov.permits_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel.state.gov.permits-14">[14]</a></sup> It is also advisable to become oriented with the driving-rules and -regulations of destination countries.<sup id="cite_ref-travel.state.gov.permits_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel.state.gov.permits-14">[14]</a></sup> Wearing a <a href="/wiki/Seat_belt" title="Seat belt">seat belt</a> is highly advisable for safety reasons; many countries have penalties for violating <a href="/wiki/Seat_belt_legislation" title="Seat belt legislation">seatbelt laws</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-travel.state.gov.permits_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travel.state.gov.permits-14">[14]</a></sup></p><p>There are three main statistics
