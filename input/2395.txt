        </div> </div></div>      </div>        <div class="page-main">                        <h1 class="page-title">                Food security in a world of natural resource scarcity      </h1>            <div class="page-content clear-block">        <div id="node-9839" class="node clear-block ntype-publication node-page">                      <div class="content clear-block">            <div class="field field-type-text field-field-pubs-subtitle">    <div class="field-items">            <div class="field-item odd">                    The role of agricultural technologies        </div>        </div></div><div class="field field-type-filefield field-field-pubs-cover-img">    <div class="field-items">            <div class="field-item odd">                    <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140305043943/http://www.ifpri.org/sites/default/files/coverimage/oc76.jpg" title="Food security in a world of natural resource scarcity" class="thickbox" rel="gallery-9839"><img src="https://web.archive.org/web/20140305043943im_/http://www.ifpri.org/sites/default/files/imagecache/thumbnail/coverimage/oc76.jpg" alt=""/></a>        </div>        </div></div><div class="node-body filter-text"><p><strong><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140305043943/http://www.ifpri.org/sites/default/files/publications/oc76.pdf">Download book (PDF 6M)</a><br/><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140305043943/http://www.ifpri.org/sites/default/files/publications/oc76app.pdf">Download appendices (PDF 16M)</a></strong></p><p>The world’s population is expected to reach 9 billion by 2050. Climate change, population, and income growth will drive food demand in the coming decades. Baseline scenarios show food prices for maize, rice, and wheat would significantly increase between 2005 and 2050, and the number of people at risk of hunger in the developing world would grow from 881 million in 2005 to more than a billion people by 2050. <em>Food Security in a World of Natural Resource Scarcity: The Role of Agricultural Technologies</em> examines which current and potential strategies offer solutions to fight hunger.</p><div class="pullquote"><h2>More information</h2><ul><li><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140305043943/http://www.ifpri.org/pressroom/briefing/promising-agricultural-technologies-feeding-world-s-poorest">Media materials</a></li><li><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140305043943/http://www.ifpri.org/event/food-security-world-growing-natural-resource-scarcity">Launch event</a> </li><li><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140305043943/http://www.ifpri.org/publication/food-security-world-natural-resource-scarcity-0">Issue Brief</a>  </li><li><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140305043943/http://apps.harvestchoice.org/agritech-toolbox/">Online Tool</a>  </li><li><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140305043943/http://www.ifpri.org/sites/default/files/infographic_agtech.jpg">Infographic</a>  </li><li><a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20140305043943/http://youtu.be/poO_UkgH9os">Video</a></li></ul></div><p>The type and effectiveness of agricultural technologies are highly debated, and the debates are often polarized. Technology options are many, but transparent evidence-based information has been inconclusive or scarce. This book endeavors to respond to the challenge of growing food sustainably without degrading our natural resource base. The authors use a groundbreaking modeling approach that combines comprehensive process-based modeling of agricultural technologies with sophisticated global food demand, supply, and trade modeling. This approach assesses the yield and food impact through 2050 of a broad range of agricultural technologies under varying assumptions of climate change for the three key staple crops: maize, rice, and wheat.</p><p>Geared toward policymakers in ministries of agriculture and national agricultural research institutes, as well as multilateral development banks and the private sector, <em>Food Security in a World of Natural Resource Scarcity</em> provides guidance on various technology strategies and which to pursue as competition grows for land, water, and energy across productive sectors and even increasingly across borders. The book is an important tool for targeting investment decisions today and going forward.</p></div><div class="field field-type-text field-field-pubs-author">      <div
