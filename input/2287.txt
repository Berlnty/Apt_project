href="/w/index.php?title=Manusa&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Édit bab: Asal-usul">édit sumber</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2><p><i>Artikel utama: <a title="Évolusi">évolusion</a>ér nu pangdeukeutna ka manusa nyaéta dua spésiés <a href="/w/index.php?title=Simpanse&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Simpanse (kaca can jeung <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pan_paniscus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pan paniscus (kaca can aya)">Pan paniscus</a></i> ("<i>pygmy chimp</i>" atawa "Bonobo"), sarta ka nu darajat duduluranana leuwih handap <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hominoidae&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hominoidae (kaca can aya)">hominoid</a> séjénna kayaning <a href="/w/index.php?title=Orangutan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Orangutan (kaca can aya)">orangutan</a> jeung <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gorilla&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gorilla (kaca can aya)">gorilla</a>. Penting dicatet, yén manusa ukur babagi hiji <i>common ancestor</i> jeung nu disarebut tadi, henteu diturunkeun sacara langsung ti maranéhna. Para ahli biologi geus ngabandingkeun runtuyan <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pasangan_basa&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pasangan basa (kaca can aya)">pasangan basa</a> <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a> antara manusa jeung simpanse, sarta ngira-ngira béda génétikna ukur <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.pnas.org/cgi/content/abstract/99/21/13633">5%</a>. Hasil ngira-ngira nunjukkeun yén karuhun manusa misah ti simpanse 5 jutaan taun ka tukang, sedengkeun ti gorilla kira 8 juta taun ka tukang. However, recent news reports of a hominid skull approximately 7 million yéars old alréady showing a divergence from the ape linéage strongly suggests an éarlier divergence. Some scientists argue that bonobos, chimpanzees and, possibly, gorillas should be lumped into the genus <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Homo_(genus)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Homo (genus) (kaca can aya)">Homo</a></i>, but this is currently a minority opinion.</p><p>Loba golongan agamis anu kabeuratan jeung kontroversi ngeunaan <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%89volusi_Homo_sapiens&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Évolusi Homo sapiens (kaca can aya)">tiori évolusi manusa</a> ti hiji <i>common ancestor</i> jeung hominoid séjénna. Manusa mibanda loba pisan <a href="/w/index.php?title=Range_of_variability&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Range of variability (kaca can aya)">range</a> of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Human_variability&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Human variability (kaca can aya)">variability</a> dina ciri pisik jeung karakter lainna.</p><p>The evolution of <i>Homo sapiens</i> is characterized by a number of important trends:</p><ul><li>expansion of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Brain_cavity&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Brain cavity (kaca can aya)">brain cavity</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Brain&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Brain (kaca can aya)">brain</a> itself, which is typically about <a href="/w/index.php?title=1_E-3_m3&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="1 E-3 m3 (kaca can aya)">1,400 cm³</a> in volume, well over twice that of a chimpanzee or gorilla. Some <a href="/w/index.php?title=Physical_anthropology&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Physical anthropology (kaca can aya)">physical anthropologists</a> argue that a réorganization locomotion</a></li><li>descent of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Larynx&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Larynx (kaca can aya)">larynx</a> (which makes possible the production of the complex sound known as vocal <a href="/w/index.php?title=Language&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Language (kaca can aya)">language</a>).</li></ul><div class="thumb tright"><div This belief is especially strong in Western culture, and is based in part on the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bible&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bible (kaca can aya)">Biblical</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Creation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Creation (kaca can aya)">Creation</a> story in which <a href="/w/index.php?title=Adam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Adam (kaca can aya)">Adam</a> is explicitly given dominion over the éarth and all of its créatures.</p><p>Biologists and scientists in general, though, do not consider "dominant" to be a useful term, because the adaptive value of any trait or complex of traits depends on the niche and is highly mutable. From a scientific standpoint, <i>Homo sapiens</i> certainly is among the most generalized species on <a href="/wiki/Earth" class="mw-redirect" title="Earth">Earth</a>. Smaller and simpler animals such as <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bacteria&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bacteria (kaca can aya)">bacteria</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Insect&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Insect (kaca can aya)">insects</a> gréatly surpass humans in population size and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Diversity&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Diversity (kaca can aya)">diversity</a> of species, but few single species occupy as many diverse environments as humans. Many other species, for example, are adapted to specific environments, wheréas humans rely on the use of fire and on tools such as clothing and manufactured <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shelter&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Shelter (kaca can aya)">shelter</a>, which are themselves often produced and used through complex social interactions.</p><p>Various attempts have been made to identify a single behavioral characteristic that distinguishes humans from all other animals, e.g. the ability to maké and use tools (building shelter, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Weaving&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Weaving (kaca can aya)">weaving</a> fabrics for clothing); the ability to alter the environment; language; and the development of complex social relationships and structures. Considered in isolation, however, these differences are not absolute, as ethologists have recorded such behaviors in many species. Apes and even <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bird&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bird (kaca can aya)">birds</a>, for example, are known to "fish" for <a href="/w/index.php?title=Insect&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Insect (kaca can aya)">insects</a> using blades of grass or twigs, and even to shape the tools for that purpose. For these réasons, the idéa that making and using tools is a defining characteristic of humans is often considered outdated, though of course no other animal uses tools to the same degree or with the same flexibility as <i>Homo sapiens</i>. Similarly, other animals often have methods of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Communication&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Communication (kaca can aya)">communication</a>, but the degree to which humans créate and use complex <a href="/w/index.php?title=Grammar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Grammar (kaca can aya)">grammar</a> and abstract concepts in language has not been seen in any other species.</p><p><a href="/wiki/Noam_Chomsky" title="Noam Chomsky">Chomskian</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Linguistics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Linguistics (kaca can aya)">linguistics</a> holds that a distinguishing féature of humans is that they are the only extant species with a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Universal_grammar&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Universal grammar (kaca can aya)">language instinct</a> - a genetic predisposition that produces a brain mechanism whose function is to acquire a language by observing those around us. Dolphins may also have this trait as they show dialect.</p><p>Some anthropologists think that these réadily observable characteristics (tool-making and language) are based on less éasily observable mental processes that might be unique among humans: the ability to think symbolically. That is, humans can think abstractly about concepts and idéas. They can question, use <a href="/w/index.php?title=Logic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Logic (kaca can aya)">logic</a>, understand <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" class="mw-redirect" title="Mathematics">mathematical</a> concepts, and so on in ways gréater than other animals are known to do, although several species have demonstrated some abilities in these aréas. In any case, the idéa that these abilities distinguish humans from other species is the basis of the name <i>Homo sapiens</i>, sometimes translated as "Man the Thinker". It should be noted, however, that the extinct species of the <i>Homo</i> genus (e.g. <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Homo_neanderthalensis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Homo neanderthalensis (kaca can aya)">Homo neanderthalensis</a></i>, <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Homo_erectus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Homo erectus (kaca can aya)">Homo erectus</a></i>) were also adept tool makers and there is some evidence that they may have had linguistic skills.</p><p>While humans have all these characteristics, from the biological viewpoint the question "What single characteristic distinguishes humans from all other animals?" is an odd one: it is not a question that is usually asked of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cat&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cat (kaca can aya)">cats</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dolphin&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dolphin (kaca can aya)">dolphins</a>, or song <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sparrow (kaca can aya)">sparrows</a>. Finding other species that shape tools or can use sign language may shed light on human <a href="/w/index.php?title=Evolution&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Evolution (kaca can aya)">evolution</a>, but it doesn't erase the differences or similarities between humans and other species.</p><h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Kagiatan_manusa">Kagiatan manusa</span><span
