class="bodytext">Generally understood as the right to feed oneself in dignity, the right to adequate food is a long-standing international human right to which many countries are committed. It is a human right of every woman, man and child that is to be fulfilled through appropriate actions by governments and non-state actors. The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development prioritizes scaled up, transformational action to eradicate poverty and end hunger and all forms of malnutrition, recognizing that permanent <a
